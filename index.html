Why this exists

Because your doctor
has 15 minutes*

You get your blood work back as a PDF. Some numbers are flagged. Your doctor glances at it, says “your cholesterol is high,” and moves on.

But nobody’s tracking your ferritin dropping for two years straight. Nobody’s connecting your fasting insulin to your sleep. Nobody asks about your light exposure, your water quality, your stress.

Take vitamin D. Your level comes back at 52 nmol/L — technically “in range.” Your doctor says nothing. But optimal is 100–150. You’re tired, your mood is off, and your labs say you’re fine. Get Based shows you the optimal range, not just the reference range. It knows you live at 55°N, work indoors, and barely get morning sun — because you told it. Context that no PDF reader and no 15-minute appointment will ever capture.
