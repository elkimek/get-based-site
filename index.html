Why this exists

Because your doctor
has 15 minutes*

You get your blood work back as a PDF. Some numbers are flagged. Your doctor glances at it, says “your cholesterol is high,” and moves on.

But nobody’s tracking your ferritin dropping for two years straight. Nobody’s connecting your fasting insulin to your sleep. Nobody asks about your light exposure, your water quality, your stress.

Take vitamin D. Your level comes back at 52 nmol/L — technically “in range.” Your doctor says nothing. But optimal is 100–150. You’re tired, your mood is off, and your labs say you’re fine. getbased shows you the optimal range, not just the reference range. It knows you live at 55°N, work indoors, and barely get morning sun — because you told it. Context that no PDF reader and no 15-minute appointment will ever capture.

Health data shouldn’t live in a drawer.

Wearables — HRV, sleep, recovery alongside your labs

Connect your ring, watch, scale, or blood pressure cuff. The signals they track — sleep, heart rate variability, recovery, blood pressure, body composition, daily activity — show up on your dashboard alongside your lab results, with a 90-day chart for every metric. When two devices measure the same thing, you pick which one drives the card. Your raw daily data stays on your device; only a small encrypted summary syncs across your phones and laptops.

Built for privacy,
not data collection

All data is stored locally in your browser. AI features send lab values to your chosen provider — personal info is always stripped first. Run a local AI model and nothing leaves your machine at all.

🔒

Nothing stored on our end

No accounts, no sign-ups. Your data lives on your device. AI features send lab values to your chosen provider — or run AI locally and stay fully offline.

Personal info is stripped from PDFs
